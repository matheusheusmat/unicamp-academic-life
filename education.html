Education

Basic Cycle (Ciclo Básico - CB)

While being a student at Unicamp, you shall be prepared to walk - A LOT. The campus is big, and you are in a city in which sun and heat are issues to be worried all year long. Of course, it varies from semester to semester, but it happens to have a class on one side of the Uni (lets abbreviate) and subsequently, another one on the other side. I strongly recommend you to have a bicycle, because it cuts the time you would spend walking in more than half. Warning given, let's talk about the classrooms.

The picture above portraits the "basic cycle" (Ciclo Básico - CB) classrooms. It is where the undergraduates have the most of their theorical classes. The first four semesters of engineering courses have calculus, physics, geometry, algebra, and other common subjects' classes in there. It is shared with other courses as well - mathematics, physics, chemistry, biology, music bachelors...

Institute of Computing (Instituto de Computação - IC)

Talking specifically about my course now, I'd like to show you the Institute of Computing (IC), which facade is in the photo above. It is one of the best places for a student of my course or of the Computer Science course to study. It has a lot of computer labs with air conditioner and, most of the times, silence. Some practical programming classes are also in there. It's a newly remodeled place, so everything there - from chairs and tables to wall paintings and computers - is new and beautiful, I should say.

The only issue with the IC is that it's located very far from the CB classrooms, on the top of a hill, whilst the rest of the Uni is in lower ground. So going there by bicycle or foot is a hard time, specially in hot days. But it provides an amazing panoramic view of the Uni and the city that surrounds it. Going there to watch the sunset is a must!

School of Electrical and Computing Engineering (Faculdade de Engenharia Elétrica e de Computação - FEEC)

The School of Electrical and Computing Engineering (Faculdade de Engenharia Elétrica e de Computação - FEEC) is another place a student of my course should be familiar with. The main entrance is shown above. The school itself is very big and consists of several building blocks: the professors' rooms block, the classrooms block, the research labs blocks, and other purposes blocks. The classrooms there are big, amphitheater-styled, not as new as the IC ones, but they serve their purpose well.

The issue with FEEC are the computer labs - the machines there are very outdated and slow, and the lack of studying places. There is no studying room, you should study in big tables located in the corridors. It's kind of hard to focus. At least there is a new lab in there, called "LED-IT", which has nice tables to study and a functional air conditioner. It is a small room, but a cool place to study if empty.
